Our approach

A four-step process, run with institutional discipline.

Conviction is earned slowly. We source proprietary, diligence relentlessly, invest with structure, and partner actively. The framework is simple; the work is not.

01 · Principles

The four we hold.

Concentrate

A small number of positions, each sized to matter. Breadth dilutes conviction.

Underwrite people

Promoters compound, not models. We underwrite the operator before the opportunity.

Build governance

We treat board composition and audit discipline as value creation, not hygiene.

Patient capital

Long holding periods. We partner with businesses through the quarters nobody celebrates.

02 · The process

Source. Diligence. Invest. Partner.

  1. 01
    Source

    Proprietary deal flow, not auctions.

    Our team spent three decades building India's SME capital-markets infrastructure. We source through merchant bankers, BSE SME listing committees, regional CA firms, and founder-to-founder referrals — relationships built before TGIsmE existed.

    Channels
    BSE SME, merchant bankers, CA network, promoter referrals
    Screen rate
    ~200 decks / year reviewed
    Signal
    Regional dominance · clean books · promoter quality
  2. 02
    Diligence

    No capital moves before the third site visit.

    Unit economics, governance readiness, and promoter-quality screens form the shortlist. Independent legal, financial, and operational reviews close the gap. Our bias is toward on-the-ground, shopfloor-level verification.

    Financial
    Audited history, quality of earnings, working-capital cycle
    Legal
    Title, licences, related-party, contingent liabilities
    Operational
    Channel checks, customer calls, site visits, hire references
  3. 03
    Invest

    Structure where it matters.

    Positions are sized to our conviction and the fund's concentration. Terms align incentives — milestone-linked deployment, anti-dilution where warranted, board rights proportionate to commitment. We don't try to out-clever good operators with term sheets.

    Sizing
    Concentrated; ranges disclosed in scheme documents
    Instruments
    Primary equity, anchor tranches, convertibles where appropriate
    Board rights
    Observer or director seats calibrated to commitment
  4. 04
    Partner

    We show up after the term sheet.

    Post-investment engagement is the real product. Board governance. CFO bench-building. Listing readiness. Warm introductions to independent directors, auditors, and institutional acquirers. We stay present for the unglamorous quarters.

    Cadence
    Monthly operating review · quarterly board
    Toolkit
    CFO hires, audit upgrades, listing path, ESOP design
    Exit
    BSE SME listing, main-board, strategic or secondary
03 · Governance

How we hold ourselves accountable.

The fund operates under SEBI's Category III AIF framework, with independent investment-committee oversight and periodic unitholder reporting.

Investment committee
Every investment and material divestment passes a formal IC review with independent voices.
Regulatory framework
SEBI Cat III AIF · Registration IN/AIF3/23-24/1473 · Scores ID aif01400.
Reporting cadence
Periodic NAV, quarterly commentary, annual audited financials. Direct investment-team access.
Conflict management
Disclosed policies on related-party transactions, co-investment, and personal trading windows.
